Education

I started my education in 2001 by attending preschool in New Hampshire. After this, I continued my actual
education in Poland, Maine (Yes, it is where they make Poland Springs water, and yes, we did have Poland Spring
water coming out of our tap for free) from grades K-12. Now I continue as a second year student here at UMaine.

I chose to attend UMaine because of it's engineering reputation. I had always wanted to become a mechanical engineer for as long as
I can remember, so I applied to school that offered engineering or were centered around that program. I applied to Roger Williams
University in Rhode Island, Wentworth Institute of Technology in Boston, Clarkson University in New York, and finally UMaine. Even
though I was accepted into all of the schools I applied for, I believed that UMaine had, by far, the best mechanical engineering
program and facilities of the four schools, so that leads to where I am now.

After my first year of college, I figured out that I didn't want to become a mechanical engineer, and that the program wasn't what
I was looking for. Being strong in math led me to changing my major to Finance in the Business College. This was a huge deal for me
because I had always told myself I wanted to become an engineer, and I ended up deciding to completely switch away from it, knowing
that it would be almost impossible to get back into the engineering college if I changed my mind. So far, I've loved every day of it.
